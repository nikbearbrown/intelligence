## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central empirical anchor — *the New Caledonian crow Mango assembling a compound tool from parts she had never seen* — or, alternatively, the four-lineage spread of tool-manufacture cases the deep-dive walks.

## Composition options

**Option A — Mango at the puzzle box.** A naturalistic illustration of a New Caledonian crow inside a research aviary, holding a partially assembled compound tool — one shaft inserted into one tube, with another shaft visible nearby. The transparent puzzle box with the food at the bottom is in the foreground. The bird's posture indicates focused attention on the assembled tool. Caption: *the compound tool was the crow's. The crow had not been trained to make it.*

**Option B — Four lineages of tool manufacture.** A field-guide composite layout with four panels. Panel one: a New Caledonian crow with a hooked twig in beak. Panel two: a Fongoli chimpanzee with a sharpened spear. Panel three: an *Amphioctopus marginatus* veined octopus stilt-walking across exposed sediment with two coconut shells suspended below its arms. Panel four: a great bowerbird with a graded-size court visible through the avenue. Caption: *four lineages, four kinds of tool, one cognitive shape — attention to a future state, manipulation to a held template, evaluation against the goal.*

**Option C — The bowerbird's perspective.** A two-panel composition. Panel one: a top-down view of a great bowerbird's bower court showing the size-graded objects from a neutral angle, with sizes plainly varying. Panel two: the same court from the female's viewing position inside the avenue, with the objects now appearing more uniform in apparent size — the forced-perspective illusion working as intended. Caption: *the male is exploiting the female's visual system. The illusion is built deliberately, restored within days when experimentally disrupted, and varies in quality between individual males.*

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of comparative-cognition figures and the field-guide composite layout.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color where possible.
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, "AI" tropes, neural-net diagrams as decoration.

## Caption (Option A)

*A New Caledonian crow assembles a compound tool from parts she has never encountered before, joining a shaft into a tube to make a stick long enough to reach the food. The behavior had not been documented in any non-human animal before 2018.*

## Caption (Option B)

*Four lineages of tool manufacture: corvids (hooked twigs), primates (sharpened spears), cephalopods (coconut-shell shelters), bowerbirds (forced-perspective bowers). Each artifact is produced by an animal attending to a future state and manipulating materials toward a goal held in mind.*

## Caption (Option C)

*The same bowerbird court, two viewpoints. Above: the objects' true sizes, visibly graded. Below: the female's view from inside the avenue, where the gradient produces a forced-perspective illusion of uniform size. The illusion is built deliberately and restored within days when experimentally disrupted.*
