# Hero image brief — Chapter 18: The Extended Mind Arrives

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** extended-mind-arrives
**Date:** 2026-04-25

---

## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central architectural claim — *every cognitive extension technology in human history extends a specific formalizable capacity while requiring an irreducible directing substrate from the human user* — and the book's arc from Chapter 1's definition problem to Chapter 18's structural close.

## Composition options

**Option A — The shelf of extensions.** A long, narrow horizontal composition showing a row of cognitive-extension technologies in chronological order, all sitting at the same height on a single illustrated shelf, each with a small caption of capacity-and-direction:

| Object | Capacity it extends | Direction it requires |
|---|---|---|
| Tally stick | Counting | What to count, why |
| Inscribed tablet | Memory | What to record |
| Antikythera mechanism / astrolabe | Astronomical prediction | When to consult |
| Movable-type press | Replication of text | What text matters |
| Microscope (van Leeuwenhoek single-lens) | Visual resolution | Distinguishing signal from artifact |
| Pocket calculator (1970s) | Arithmetic | Problem formulation |
| Bomb-sniffing dog (paired with handler silhouette) | Olfactory detection | Threat assessment |
| GPS receiver | Spatial localization | Goal-setting |
| Recommendation engine (small server icon) | Reward prediction at scale | Specifying the right reward (Goodhart) |
| Generative AI (transformer-block icon) | Pattern recognition + generation | All of the above, layered |

The composition's caption: *every entry sits at the same height. The capacity has gotten broader. The directing role has not vanished.*

**Option B — Otto's notebook and Rex's nose.** A two-panel naturalistic composition. Left panel: Otto sitting on a park bench in coat and hat (the Clark-Chalmers thought experiment, evoking Alzheimer's), notebook open in his lap, writing something down with a small museum visible in the background. Right panel: Rex the bomb-sniffing dog from Ch. 7 working a checkpoint, his handler's hand on the leash, dog's head down, sniffing a vehicle's wheel well. Above both panels, a single arrow labeled "directing layer" pointing from the human in each panel down to the tool/animal. Caption: *the notebook and the dog do different things on different substrates. They sit in the same architectural relationship to the human using them.*

**Option C — The full book in one diagram.** A circular diagram. At the center: the question mark from the book's title, in large type. Around the center, in a ring: the seventeen chapters' capacities (steering, learning, emotion, pattern recognition, navigation, prediction, simulation, social cognition, theory of mind, creativity, self-awareness, metacognition, language, collective intelligence, AI synthesis). Outside the ring, in a second concentric arc: the fourteen Extension Notes (pH meter, Roomba, writing, sentiment AI, microscope, GPS/dogs, recommendation engines, simulators, social networks, A/B testing, generative AI, mirrors/cameras, confidence intervals, internet, institutions). Outside that: a single dotted-line label, *the directing layer,* with arrows pointing inward to every capacity. Caption: *the book in one figure. Each chapter a capacity. Each chapter's Extension Note a tool. The question mark at the center is what the book defended.*

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration with diagrammatic elements.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color where possible.
- For Option A, the row should look like an actual museum shelf — wood-grain shelf, neutral lighting, restrained labels.
- For Option C, the circular diagram should feel like a frontispiece for the book — clean, minimal, weighted.
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, "AI" tropes, neural-net diagrams as decoration.

## Caption (Option A)

*The shelf of cognitive-extension technologies, from the tally stick to the transformer. Every entry sits at the same height because every entry has the same architectural shape: a specific formalizable capacity, offloaded to a substrate that does it better than biology, paired with a specific non-formalizable directing layer that the human user retains. AI is the latest entry on the shelf. The shelf is not new.*

## Caption (Option B)

*Otto and his notebook (Clark-Chalmers' 1998 thought experiment for the extended mind thesis) and Rex the bomb-sniffing dog at a checkpoint (Chapter 7). The two cognitive-extension partnerships look nothing alike at the level of substrate — paper and ink versus a 300-million-receptor olfactory system. They sit in the same architectural relationship to the human user. The notebook stores what Otto records; it does not decide what to record. The dog signals the smell; he does not assess whether the smell is dangerous. The directing layer is the human, in both cases.*

## Caption (Option C)

*The book in one figure. The seventeen capacities the book has cataloged ring the central question mark of the title. Each chapter's Extension Note — the technology that extends the capacity beyond biological limits — sits in the outer arc. The dotted line that surrounds everything is the directing layer: the human work that the technologies do not do, and that makes the technologies useful when they are useful and dangerous when they are not. The question mark at the center is what the book has been defending. Intelligence is plural. The architecture is conserved.*
