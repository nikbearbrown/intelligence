# Hero image brief — Chapter 7: Navigation and Spatial Intelligence

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** navigation

## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central architectural argument — *the hippocampal map made of place cells and grid cells* — or, alternatively, the chapter's hook scene of the bomb-sniffing dog at the checkpoint.

## Composition options

**Option A — The grid cell tiling.** A schematic showing, on the floor of a small experimental arena, the firing fields of a single grid cell rendered as red dots arranged in a hexagonal lattice. Adjacent panels show two more grid cells with the same hexagonal spacing but offset phase, illustrating the population code. A small inset shows the rat trajectory through the arena. Caption: *the brain has a coordinate system implemented in cortical tissue*.

**Option B — Rex at the checkpoint.** A naturalistic illustration of a Belgian Malinois in front of a vehicle at a checkpoint, in the alert posture (sitting, fixed gaze on the target spot below the bumper). The handler is in the foreground holding the lead. Caption: *the dog is not a sensor; the dog is an entire navigation system*.

**Option C — The phylogenetic spread.** A stylized illustration with five small panels showing the same conceptual problem (find a target across distance) being solved by five different species: a Cataglyphis ant on featureless saltpan navigating by sky compass, a Clark's nutcracker triangulating a cache location from distal landmarks, a Pacific salmon homing on a magnetic-coordinate signature, a London taxi driver tracing a route through Soho on a printed mental map, and the bomb-sniffing dog at the checkpoint. Caption: *one architecture, six sensory frontends*.

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of journal-figure aesthetics for Option A and Option B and field-guide composite layout for Option C.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color where possible.
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, neural-net diagrams as decoration.

## Caption (Option A)

*Grid cells in the medial entorhinal cortex fire in a hexagonal lattice across the surface of any environment the animal moves through. Different grid cells share the same lattice spacing but are offset in phase, so the activity pattern across the population uniquely identifies position. This is a coordinate system, in cortical tissue.*

## Caption (Option B)

*A Belgian Malinois at a vehicle checkpoint, in the alert posture. The detection looks like a single moment. Underneath is a hippocampal map of where he has searched, an internal model of how scent plumes behave in moving air, and a behavioral protocol for telling his handler what he found.*

## Caption (Option C)

*Six species, six sensory frontends, one architecture. The hippocampal map system is half a billion years old; what changes between lineages is what the map is built out of — sky compass, magnetic field, scent plume, paper streets — and what the species needs to do with it.*
