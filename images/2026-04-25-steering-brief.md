# Hero image brief — Chapter 3: Steering

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** steering
**Date:** 2026-04-25

## Concept

A composition that visualizes the worm's two big additions to bacterial chemotaxis: the *labeled-line sensory array at the head* and the *neuromodulator layer* that sets the gain on each line. The reader should see, at a glance, that *Caenorhabditis* is the bacterium-of-Chapter-2 with a head full of named sensors and a slow chemical layer underneath the synaptic activity.

## Composition options

**Option A — Anatomical plate of the head.** A natural-history-style anatomical illustration of the *C. elegans* head from above and from the side, with the major amphid sensory neurons labeled by name (ASE, AWA, AWC, ASH, AFD, ADL, ASI, ASJ, ASK, ASG). Each label paired with a one-word note: *salt*, *attractants*, *repellents*, *nociception*, *temperature*. A small inset on the side shows the nerve ring and the ventral cord; another small inset shows a worm in mid-pirouette with the body bent into the omega shape. Honor the lineage of the WormAtlas plates and the original Pierce-Shimomura figures.

**Option B — The food-copper trade-off.** A schematic of the integration assay: petri dish viewed from above, diacetyl smear on one side, copper sulfate barrier across the middle, two worm tracks. One labeled *fed*, retreating from the copper; the other labeled *starved*, crossing through to the food. A faint side-bar labels which neurons are firing in each case (AWA for diacetyl, ASH for copper, AIY/AIB for integration). The visual argument is that the same animal makes different choices under different internal states.

**Option C — The neuromodulator layer.** Cross-section of the worm with the synaptic connectome rendered as a dense black graph and the neuromodulator distribution rendered as a soft chemical cloud overlaid in two colors (dopamine and serotonin) — visually showing that the "wireless" chemical layer modulates the entire wired circuit. Caption: *the worm has two nervous systems — the wired one and the chemical one*.

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of the WormAtlas anatomical plates.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color (yellow or sepia).
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, neural-net diagrams as decoration, "AI" tropes.
- Scale-bar markers where the actual size of the animal matters (1 mm length, ~80 µm head diameter).

## Caption (Option A)

*Sixty sensory neurons, twelve pairs in the amphid, each tuned to a class of stimulus and labeled with a default valence. The first nervous system in this book is also the first one we have completely mapped.*

## Caption (Option B)

*A worm at a copper barrier. The same animal makes different decisions when fed and when starved. The internal state — encoded in slow neuromodulators that set the gain on every sensory channel — is the difference.*

## Caption (Option C)

*Two nervous systems, in the same animal. The wired connectome (5,000 chemical synapses) carries fast information; the chemical neuromodulator layer carries internal state on a slower timescale. Both are needed to explain how the worm decides.*
