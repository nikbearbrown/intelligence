**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)

## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central claim — *procedural uncertainty monitoring is conserved across radically different brain architectures, with the species set narrowing as one climbs Pearl's ladder of causation*.

## Composition options

**Option A — The phylogenetic spread of opt-out.** A field-guide composite layout with five panels. Panel one: the dolphin Natua at the auditory paddle setup, in mid-press of the third (uncertainty) paddle, with the tone-source visible above the water. Panel two: a rhesus macaque in front of a touchscreen, choosing the "decline" icon over a 4-way memory test. Panel three: a rat at a duration-discrimination chamber, exiting through the opt-out tunnel. Panel four: a honeybee at a Y-maze choice point, walking out the exit tunnel rather than choosing between two ambiguous symmetry stimuli. Panel five: a western scrub-jay at a peephole, peering at the hidden-baiting site before approaching the food cache. Each panel labeled with species, year, and the dimension along which difficulty was varied. Caption: *five lineages, five sensory modalities, one operational signature — the agent's behavior gates on its own internal certainty*.

**Option B — Pearl's ladder, climbed in the head.** A three-tier vertical composition, with a different species at each rung. Bottom rung (Association): a honeybee at the exit tunnel, with arrow-text "internal certainty → external accuracy." Middle rung (Intervention): a scrub-jay at the peephole, arrow-text "act on the world to change one's own knowledge." Top rung (Counterfactual): a chimpanzee mid-pause in front of a baited container she has just been allowed to inspect, arrow-text "what would I have done if I had not seen the baiting?" with the top rung partially faded to indicate the evidential thinness for non-human counterfactual self-reflection. Caption: *Pearl's three rungs of causal reasoning, applied to one's own cognition. The species set narrows as one climbs.*

**Option C — Hampton's apparatus.** A naturalistic illustration of the rhesus macaque memory-monitoring setup. The macaque sits at a touchscreen. The screen shows a target image (panel 1, study phase), then a delay (panel 2), then the choice phase (panel 3) where the macaque has two options: take a four-image memory test, or decline for a small guaranteed reward. The macaque's hand hovers over the decline option after a long delay. Caption: *the macaque declines the memory test when memory has faded. The image was the same. The delay was the same. What varied was the macaque's internal mnemonic state.*

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of comparative-cognition figures from journal papers.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color where possible.
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, "AI" tropes, neural-net diagrams as decoration.
- For Option B, the rung diagram should look like a ladder drawn with biological care — not a generic infographic.

## Caption (Option A)

*Five opt-out passers across radically different anatomies and 600-plus million years of evolution. Each panel shows the same operational signature: an agent that modulates its behavior based on a difficulty parameter the experimenter cannot read off any external stimulus feature. The function is conserved. The substrate is not.*

## Caption (Option B)

*Pearl's three rungs of causal reasoning, applied to one's own cognition. Bottom rung — associate internal certainty with external accuracy. Middle rung — intervene on one's own knowledge state. Top rung — reason about what one would have done under alternative conditions. The species set narrows as one climbs. The clearest cases at the top are human.*

## Caption (Option C)

*Hampton's 2001 memory-monitoring apparatus. The macaque sees a target, waits, and then chooses between a memory test (large reward if right, no reward if wrong) and a decline option (small guaranteed reward). After short delays, the macaque takes the test. After long delays, it declines. The image was the same. The delay was the same. What varied was the macaque's internal mnemonic state — and the macaque acted on it.*
