# Hero image brief — Chapter 17: AI as Data Point

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** ai-as-data-point

## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central claim — *current AI produces an unfamiliar profile across the book's axes, with extreme highs on some axes and near-zero on others, unlike any biological organism's profile* — or, alternatively, the Geirhos cat-with-elephant-skin diagnostic that opens the chapter.

## Composition options

**Option A — The radar chart of selves.** A composite figure showing four overlaid radar plots ("spider charts") on the same axes. The axes are the fourteen capacities the book has built (steering, learning, emotion, pattern recognition, navigation, prediction, simulation, social cognition, theory of mind, creativity, self-awareness, metacognition, language, collective intelligence). Plot one: chimpanzee profile. Plot two: octopus profile. Plot three: human profile. Plot four (in a contrasting line color): current frontier AI. The AI profile reaches further out than any biological profile on prediction / pattern-recognition / language / retrieval, and falls all the way to or near the center on navigation / metacognition / theory of mind / cumulative-culture / stakes. Caption: *the AI profile is unlike any biological organism's profile. The shape, not the height, is the chapter's observation.*

**Option B — The cat with elephant skin.** A two-panel naturalistic-with-diagrammatic composition. Left panel: the Geirhos 2018 cat-with-elephant-texture image, with two outputs labeled — "human: cat" and "ImageNet ResNet: elephant." Right panel: the inverse, an elephant-with-cat-fur image, with the labels swapped. Below, a small histogram showing the cat-vs-elephant cue conflict response distribution from the Geirhos paper, with the human distribution centered on shape and the network distribution centered on texture. Caption: *a 2018 diagnostic. The network reached human-level accuracy on ImageNet by classifying textures, not objects. The same external behavior; a different cognition underneath.*

**Option C — The shadow phylogeny.** A field-guide layout showing the existing tree of biological cognition (bacterium → slime mold → worm → fish → reptile → bird → mammal → primate → human) along the lower half of the page, with selected passers from the previous chapters (cleaner wrasse mark-test, scrub-jay metacognition, dolphin opt-out, chimpanzee false-belief tracking, human child declarative pointing). Above the biological tree, in a contrasting style (perhaps dashed-line silhouettes), an artificial branch — vision transformer, large language model, embodied robot — labeled with the dates of their introduction (2012 AlexNet, 2017 Transformer, 2022 ChatGPT, 2025 frontier multimodal agent). The biological tree's branches all point to a converging set of capacities; the artificial branch points to a different set with different reach. The two trees do not connect. Caption: *the artificial branch is not a continuation of the biological tree. It is a separate lineage with its own selection pressures.*

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration with diagrammatic elements.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color where possible.
- For Option A, the radar plots should be drawn with care — the AI line should be one consistent contrasting color across all four overlaid charts, so it is visible at a glance.
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, "AI" tropes, neural-net diagrams as decoration.

## Caption (Option A)

*Cognitive profiles across fourteen capacities. The chimpanzee, octopus, and human each occupy a recognizable region of the space. The current frontier AI does not — it reaches further out on a few axes than any biological organism and falls to or near the center on others. The shape of the placement is unlike any profile in the biological catalog. The chapter argues that this shape, not a single height, is the honest answer to "where AI sits."*

## Caption (Option B)

*A 2018 diagnostic from the Tübingen group. An ImageNet-trained convolutional network shown a cat re-textured with elephant skin reports "elephant." Shown an elephant re-textured with cat fur, it reports "cat." The network had reached human-level accuracy on ImageNet by classifying textures, not objects. The same external benchmark performance; a different cognitive process underneath.*

## Caption (Option C)

*The biological tree of cognition is the territory the previous sixteen chapters have surveyed. The artificial branch above it is not a continuation. It is a separate lineage selected by gradient descent rather than by survival, trained on the output of the biological tree rather than subjected to its costs. The shape of the artificial branch is its own.*
