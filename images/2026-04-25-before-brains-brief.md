# Hero image brief — Chapter 2: Before Brains

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** before-brains
**Date:** 2026-04-25

---

## Concept

A composition that sets the chapter's claim — that decisions occur in cells, networks, and plants without any neuron — by showing three radically different aneural deciders side by side, each in mid-decision, each labeled with the substrate on which the decision lives.

## Composition options

**Option A — Three deciders.** A clean three-panel illustration:

1. *E. coli*, two micrometers long, mid-run with flagella bundled and rotating CCW. Caption: "memory in two enzymes."
2. *Physarum polycephalum*, mid-experiment in a maze, with the shortest path between two food sources rendered as a thick yellow tube and the dead-end pseudopodia rendered fainter. Caption: "memory in a slime trail."
3. *Dionaea muscipula* (Venus flytrap), one trigger hair just touched, the other untouched, with the calcium concentration in the trap visualized as a faint colored gradient. Caption: "memory in calcium."

A small typeset rule below each panel: *sensing → memory → integration → variable response*, with the relevant biological substrate underlined for each organism.

**Option B — The bacterial deep-dive alone.** A schematic of the chemotaxis molecular cascade — MCP receptor in the membrane, CheA kinase, CheY-P diffusing to the flagellar motor, CheR and CheB modifying receptor methylation. The 4-second weighting window shown as a small inset graph: past second positive, prior 3 seconds negative, response proportional to the area difference. Honors the math-on-the-page rule visually.

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of Haeckel's plates and the early *Nature* line drawings.
- Limited palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color (yellow for *Physarum*, perhaps).
- Avoid: glowing-brain iconography, neural-net diagrams, "AI" tropes, decorative noise.
- Where possible, scale-bar markers to give the reader a sense of how small or large each organism is.

## Caption (Option A)

*Three deciders, no neurons. The bacterium remembers in enzyme states; the slime mold remembers in trails; the flytrap remembers in calcium. Each implements the same minimum architecture — sensing, memory, integration, variable response — in a different substrate.*

## Caption (Option B)

*Memory in two enzymes. The bacterial chemotaxis cascade computes a 4-second temporal derivative of attractant concentration: the past second has positive weight, the prior three are negative, and the cell suppresses tumbling when the difference is positive. Diagram after Berg, Brown, Segall, Block, and the literature that followed.*
