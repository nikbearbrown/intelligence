# Hero image brief — Chapter 1: The Definition Problem

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** what-is-intelligence
**Date:** 2026-04-25 (rewrite)

---

## Concept

A composition that visualizes *the definition problem itself*: many partial views of the same object, each accurate, none complete. The natural-history register asks for honest scientific illustration rather than abstract iconography.

## Composition options (pick one)

**Option A — The Many Sieves.** Five labeled sieves of progressively finer mesh — *Binet*, *Wechsler*, *Gardner*, *Legg-Hutter*, *Wissner-Gross* — arrayed in a row. A miscellaneous handful of organisms (worm, bee, fish, crow, octopus, dog, person, chess engine, bacterium colony, language model interface) is being passed through them. Different organisms fall through at different sieves. The viewer should see at a glance that the answer to "what counts as intelligent" depends on which sieve you are using.

**Option B — Anatomical-plate composite.** A natural-history-style plate (Haeckel-adjacent register) showing a small selection of organisms — bacterium, *C. elegans*, bumblebee, fish, octopus, crow, dog, human, with a small chess board or screen rendering the AI cases — each labeled with its taxonomic name and a short phrase about what kind of cognitive work it is doing. The visual argument is that all of these belong in one frame; the book is asking what we mean when we use one word to span this range.

**Option C — Two-dozen-definitions visualization.** A circular arrangement of 24 short text fragments — each one a definition of intelligence drawn from the literature, attribution beneath. The viewer's eye is forced to move across many partial views, none authoritative. Reads as a kind of scholarly mandala. Honest about the chapter's argument: the field has not converged.

## Style

- Natural-history register. Honor the lineage of Audubon, Haeckel, E.O. Wilson's plates.
- Clean linework, restrained palette, scientific labels.
- Avoid: glowing-brain iconography, humanoid robots, neural-net diagrams as decoration, "AI" tropes.

## Caption (Option A version)

*Five sieves, one set of candidates. A definition of intelligence is not a label but a sieve — what falls through it determines which organisms, and which tools, we agree to call intelligent. The book commits to the third one over.*

## Caption (Option B version)

*Each labeled organism is doing some kind of cognitive work in some kind of environment. Whether the work counts as intelligence depends on which definition you are using to ask.*

## Note

Do not generate the image automatically. Brief is for human image direction. My recommendation: **Option B**, because it sets the natural-history register the book's voice promises and gives a reader the cast of characters they will meet across the next sixteen chapters.
