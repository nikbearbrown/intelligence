# Hero image brief — Chapter 9: Simulation and Planning

**Book:** intelligence (*Intelligence? A Natural History of Mind Across the Animal Kingdom*)
**Chapter slug:** simulation-and-planning
**Date:** 2026-04-25

## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central claim — *the hippocampus running a fast trajectory through space the animal has not yet walked* — by showing forward and reverse replay sweeps as compressed temporal events overlaid on the spatial map.

## Composition options

**Option A — Forward and reverse replay over a maze map.** A schematic top-down view of an open arena with a known goal location marked. Two trajectory traces overlaid. The forward-replay trace, in one color, runs *before movement* from the rat's current location to the goal — depicted as a fast compressed sweep marked by a small "100 ms" timing label. The reverse-replay trace, in another color, runs *after the rat reaches the goal* in the opposite direction — labeled "reward then reverse sweep". Below, a small spike-raster shows the place-cell sequences in fast compressed time during a sharp-wave ripple. Caption: *the hippocampus is a trajectory simulator*.

**Option B — Restaurant Row regret.** A top-down view of the circular Restaurant Row arena with four spokes for the four restaurants. A rat trajectory shown skipping the chocolate restaurant (good deal at 2 sec) and arriving at cherry (bad deal at 30 sec). At cherry, the rat's posture indicates the look-back. A small inset shows OFC neurons firing in the pattern they had previously fired in *at the chocolate restaurant*. Caption: *the rat's brain represents the action it should have taken — not the missed reward, the missed action*.

**Option C — The compression.** A timeline visual showing the actual rat trajectory taking ~10 seconds to traverse, paired with the forward-replay sweep depicting the same trajectory in ~100 milliseconds — a hundredfold compression. The visual makes the *speed* of mental rehearsal vivid: thinking through an action is roughly two orders of magnitude faster than performing it.

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of the original Pfeiffer-Foster figures and Steiner-Redish raster panels.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-two-colors (one for forward, one for reverse).
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, "AI" tropes, neural-net diagrams as decoration.

## Caption (Option A)

*Forward replay before movement; reverse replay after reward. The same hippocampal place cells that map the animal's current location during real movement run in fast compressed sequences during the gaps — sweeping forward to plan future paths, backward to credit reward to past steps. The neural substrate of mental simulation is the same substrate that, milliseconds earlier, was tracking actual position.*

## Caption (Option B)

*A rat at the cherry restaurant after skipping the chocolate one. The look-back is the behavioral signature; the OFC firing pattern is the neural one. The neurons that fire during the look-back are the neurons that fired earlier at the chocolate restaurant — the brain is representing the unchosen option, not the disappointing current one.*

## Caption (Option C)

*A trajectory the rat ran in ten seconds, replayed in one hundred milliseconds. Mental rehearsal is roughly a hundredfold faster than physical performance. This is the compression that makes simulation a usable tool rather than a luxury.*
