## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central convergence claim — *complex sociality has evolved on anatomically distinct brains in primates, cetaceans, and elephants, with at least one cellular convergence in the form of Von Economo neurons* — by showing the three lineages side by side with the shared cellular adaptation rendered explicitly.

## Composition options

**Option A — The convergence triptych.** Three panels arranged horizontally. Panel one: a chimpanzee group in a fission-fusion gathering, two grooming, one watching. Panel two: a pod of dolphins in a multi-male alliance configuration. Panel three: an elephant family group with a matriarch at the center. Below each panel, a small inset shows the same cell type — the spindle-shaped Von Economo neuron — with anatomically appropriate variation and the same simplified dendritic morphology. Caption: *three lineages, three brains, one cell type. Complex sociality selects for the same cellular adaptation across phylogenetic distances of tens to hundreds of millions of years.*

**Option B — The chimp competitive paradigm.** A schematic top-down view of the Hare-Call-Tomasello apparatus. A subordinate chimpanzee on one side, a dominant on the other, with two food items between them. One food item visible only to the subordinate, one visible to both. Arrows show which food the subordinate goes for under each configuration. Caption: *the subordinate is not running for the closest food. The subordinate is modeling what the dominant can see and acting accordingly.*

**Option C — The dog-human gaze.** A naturalistic illustration of a dog and a human in mutual gaze, with a small annotated chemical inset showing the oxytocin pathway in both species. The inset notes that the same loop is absent in human-wolf pairs. Caption: *the dog has hijacked a mammalian bonding mechanism that did not evolve in its lineage and made it run cross-species. Ten thousand years of selection produced an inter-species oxytocin loop.*

## Caption (Option A)

*The function shapes the substrate. Three socially-complex mammalian lineages — primates, cetaceans, elephants — separated by tens to hundreds of millions of years and built on anatomically distinct brains, all evolved Von Economo neurons in the anterior cingulate cortex and frontal insula. The cellular convergence tells us something deep about the computational shape of complex sociality.*

## Caption (Option B)

*A subordinate chimpanzee competes with a dominant. When food is positioned so only the subordinate can see it, the subordinate goes for it. When the dominant can see, the subordinate retreats. The chimp is tracking the dominant's visual perspective and acting on the model.*

## Caption (Option C)

*A dog and a human in mutual gaze. Across the ten-to-twenty-thousand years of canine domestication, an inter-species oxytocin loop has emerged in dogs that does not exist in human-raised wolves. Social attunement is sometimes a domain-specific adaptation rather than a general cognitive capacity.*
