## Concept

A composition that visualizes the chapter's central claim — *visual self-recognition is conserved across phylogenetically distant lineages on radically different brain anatomies* — by showing several mirror-test passers side by side, each in mid-mark-test, with the marked spot rendered as a small visual highlight.

## Composition options

**Option A — The phylogenetic spread.** A field-guide composite layout with five panels showing mirror-test passers in mid-test: a chimpanzee touching her eyebrow ridge while looking at her reflection; an Asian elephant (Happy) touching the painted X on her head with her trunk; a bottlenose dolphin orienting her body sideways to view a mark; a magpie (Pica pica) scratching at a colored sticker on its black throat feathers; a cleaner wrasse (Labroides dimidiatus) scraping a brown throat-mark against a rock. Each panel labeled with species, year of test passage, and lab. Caption: *five lineages, five anatomies, one operational test passed in each*.

**Option B — The fish in the mirror.** A naturalistic underwater illustration of the cleaner wrasse experimental setup. A small fish in mid-scrape against a rock, with the brown throat-mark rendered visible. Behind the fish, the mirror reflects the action. Caption: *the cleaner wrasse weighs a few grams. Its brain weighs less than a hundred milligrams. It scrapes the mark only when the mirror is present.*

**Option C — The four Gallup stages.** A four-panel sequence showing a single chimpanzee progressing through Gallup's stages: panel 1, social display at the reflection; panel 2, physical inspection of the mirror; panel 3, contingency testing (sticking out the tongue while watching); panel 4, self-directed behavior (using the mirror to investigate the ear). Below, a small mark-test inset showing the chimp touching the marked spot. Caption: *Gallup's four-stage progression. Each stage is a behavioral signature that the animal is using the reflection in a more sophisticated way.*

## Style

- Natural-history scientific illustration. Honor the lineage of comparative-cognition figures from journal papers.
- Restrained palette. Black-and-white-and-one-color where possible.
- Avoid: glowing-brain icons, "AI" tropes, neural-net diagrams as decoration.

## Caption (Option A)

*Five mirror-test passers across radically different anatomies and 400-plus million years of evolution. Each panel shows the mark-test signature: the animal using the mirror to investigate a part of its body it cannot otherwise see. The function is conserved. The substrate is not.*

## Caption (Option B)

*A finger-sized cleaner wrasse scrapes a brown mark off its throat against a rock after seeing the mark in the mirror. The behavior occurs only when the mirror is present, only when the mark is visible to the wrasse's species, and only when the spot is one the fish cannot see directly. By every behavioral signature Gallup designed in 1970 to detect self-recognition in chimpanzees, the wrasse passes the test.*

## Caption (Option C)

*Gallup's four-stage progression. The chimpanzee first treats the reflection as a stranger, then explores the mirror as an object, then tests the contingency between her movements and the reflection's movements, and finally uses the mirror to investigate parts of her own body she cannot otherwise see. The mark test is the controlled validation: she touches the painted spot only when the mirror is present.*
