## Concept

A preface is not a chapter. It is a writer-to-reader handshake about what the book is, what it is not, and how to read it. The four-move pedagogical arc and 2,000–4,000-word ceiling do not apply. Target length ~1,200 words.

## What the preface does

1. **Opens with the cast of characters.** Six images of cognition in different organisms (nematode, honeybee, bumblebee, crow, dog, octopus) plus one of an LLM, each in a single concrete sentence with primary-source citation where the claim is contestable. The cast is the cast that will populate the rest of the book.
2. **Names the question mark.** Why the title has one and not a period. Frames the book's posture: not a verdict on what intelligence *is*, but a survey of what it does in different organisms under different definitions.
3. **States what the book will do.** Phylogenetic walk through capacities, asking the same set of questions of each.
4. **States what the book will not do.** Will not settle whether bees feel pessimism the way humans feel sadness, whether dogs are conscious, whether LLMs understand. Posture is to show evidence and let the reader decide.
5. **Names the structural device.** Each chapter ends with an Extension Note. The Extension Notes accumulate without commentary until the leitmotif is loud enough to name (Ch. 18).
6. **Sets voice expectations.** Attenborough × Feynman. Math on the page where math is the argument. First person to label position-taking.
7. **Names the audience.** Readers of de Waal, Godfrey-Smith, Annie Murphy Paul, Bennett.
8. **Hands off to Chapter 1.**

## Voice notes

- More direct address than a chapter. "I" appears five times, "you" appears throughout. The preface is a handshake, not a lecture.
- Cast-of-characters opening is six single-sentence-ish paragraphs followed by a single-sentence pivot ("None of these facts fits cleanly..."). Single-sentence paragraphs used as weapons at three or four pivots, per CLAUDE.md §6.
- No Learning Objectives, no Prerequisites, no "Where this chapter fits."
- No "What would change my mind," no "Still puzzling." These don't fit a preface; they're chapter conventions.
- Suggested titles + Tags retained.

## What the preface does not do

- Does not commit to a definition. That is Ch. 1's job. Preface only previews that the commitment is coming.
- Does not survey the candidates. That is also Ch. 1's job.
- Does not include an Extension Note. Extension Notes start in Ch. 2 per the book brief.
- Does not provide a hero image brief or a `/mega` exercise set. Prefaces don't carry those.
